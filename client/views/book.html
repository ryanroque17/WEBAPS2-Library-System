Another great book from John Green. Surprised at the in depth handling of mental issues. It conveys the troubles very well. The book is more centered on the protagonist's thoughts than the adventure in itself. All John Green's typical elements are present: mystery, cars, death, relationships, weird names, social media and interesting facts about the universe. If you liked his other books, you will certainly love this one.

Wed, 25 Mar

Another brilliantly written book by one of my favorite authors. Turtles All the Way Down is an unprecedented look at teen mental illness, friendship, love and life that manages to be both funny and heartbreaking, and educational too. I read the book in one sitting, stopped to cry and laugh a few times, peppered it with sticky notes for passages to revisit and I am certain I will be re-reading it soon. I couldn't have asked for more from a book, except for it not to end. Thank you, John Green, for sharing such a personal glimpse into your head, and for helping those who struggle to feel more normal.

Sat, 5 Mar

No traditional review of this one…..I just can’t. I got it at school at 11:40 am on release day and finished it at 6:25 pm and it BROKE. MY. HEART. It’s the most powerful and open book about mental illness that I have read, and it’s required reading for everyone, but especially those who don’t understand the intensity of OCD and extreme anxiety. It is unlike any of his other work, but it’s still funny and it’s still so so sad, but it’s also Green’s OWN struggle. Go read his interview with NYT and try to tell me you didn’t want to cry. And if I see even ONE “but it’s not like TFIOS” whiny review…….don’t get me started. Read this book. Work through the discomfort. Honor his pain.

About the Author
John Green is the award-winning, #1 bestselling author of Looking for Alaska, An Abundance of Katherines, Paper Towns, Will Grayson, Will Grayson (with David Levithan), and The Fault in Our Stars. His many accolades include the Printz Medal, a Printz Honor, and the Edgar Award. John has twice been a finalist for the LA Times Book Prize and was selected by TIME magazine as one of the 100 Most Influential People in the World. With his brother, Hank, John is one half of the Vlogbrothers and co-created the online educational series CrashCourse. You can join the millions who follow him on Twitter @johngreen and Instagram @johngreenwritesbooks or visit him online at johngreenbooks.com. John lives with his family in Indianapolis, Indiana.
